REASON WITH YOUR FRIEND.

“These people are not protectors, they are the ones we need protecting from!” you say.

Your friend is not convinced. “Can’t you see, , there’s nothing left for us here!”

“And that’s because of these bandits!” you cry frustratedly. “Please, you have to trust me. Don’t you trust me?”

These words hit your friend. He hesitates visibly, and then his face becomes determined. “Duck,” he mutters.

As you duck, your friend throws his dagger at the nearest bandit.

As the bandit howls in pain, you and your friend run towards the stables.

You both jump on your horses and ride past the bandits, avoiding arrows flying next to you.

“Where do we go,” your friend asks, out of breath.

“I saw the villagers running west, towards the sea,” you say.

After hours of breathless riding, your friend asks to take a break.
